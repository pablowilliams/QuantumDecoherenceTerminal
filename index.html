    Abstract
    
            
    This dashboard implements a Lindblad master-equation inference layer over the global fleet of gate-model and analog quantum processors. The Lindblad open-quantum-system formalism (Lindblad, 1976) provides the canonical noise model for superconducting, trapped-ion, and neutral-atom qubits, parameterized by relaxation T1 and dephasing T2 (Nielsen & Chuang, 2010). Per-platform T1, T2, and two-qubit gate fidelity are seeded from manufacturer disclosures and peer-reviewed publications including the Willow below-threshold-QEC demonstration (Acharya et al., 2024) and Cross et al.'s Quantum Volume benchmark (Cross et al., 2019). Pauli-channel approximations were rejected because they cannot reproduce the geometric Bloch-sphere intuition critical for the visualization layer.
    

            
    Stochastic model
    
            
    A single qubit interacting with a noisy environment loses quantum coherence on two characteristic timescales: T1 (energy relaxation; population decay from |1⟩ to |0⟩) and T2 (phase coherence loss). The two are linked by 1/T2 = 1/(2T1) + 1/Tφ where Tφ is the pure dephasing time. T2 ≤ 2T1 is a fundamental physical bound. For useful computation we need T2 to vastly exceed the gate operation time.
    
      
    The dynamics of a qubit's density matrix ρ under both unitary Hamiltonian H and decoherence is the Lindblad master equation:
    
      
    dρ/dt = −i [H, ρ] + (1/T1) D[σ_−] ρ + (1/(2 Tφ)) D[σ_z] ρ
    
      
    The Lindblad dissipator is D[L]ρ = LρL† − ½{L†L, ρ}; σ_− drives relaxation and σ_z drives pure dephasing. The Bloch vector (x, y, z) = (⟨σ_x⟩, ⟨σ_y⟩, ⟨σ_z⟩) evolves under the Bloch equations:
    
      
    dx/dt = −x/T2,  dy/dt = −y/T2,  dz/dt = −(z − z_eq)/T1
    
      
    where z_eq is the equilibrium population difference (typically z_eq = 1 for a cold qubit). The Bloch vector magnitude |r| shrinks toward the z-axis under pure dephasing and toward the equilibrium z-pole under relaxation. A coherent state has |r| = 1 (pure state on the Bloch sphere surface); a maximally mixed state has |r| = 0 (center of the sphere).
    
      
    For a circuit of depth d with per-gate time τ_g, total runtime is τ_total = d · τ_g. The leading-order fidelity loss is 1 − F ≈ τ_total · (1/T1 + 1/(2T2)). Setting this ≤ 0.5 gives the gate budget: d_max ≈ 0.5 / (τ_g · (1/T1 + 1/(2T2))). For Heron r2 with T1 = 250 μs, T2 = 200 μs, τ_g = 100 ns this gives d_max ≈ 670 gates per single-qubit register — but two-qubit gates have fidelity floors (currently 99.5–99.9%) that dominate the budget in practice.
    
      
    Quantum Volume (Cross et al. 2019) is a holistic benchmark: the largest square circuit (depth = width) for which the QPU can compute heavy-output probability above the threshold. QV doubles every ~9 months on the leading platforms (Heron, Willow, H2). For neutral-atom analog systems QV is not directly meaningful and we use circuit-layer-fidelity as a proxy.
    

            
    Calibration
    
            
    Per-QPU T1, T2, gate time, and 2-qubit gate fidelity are calibrated from manufacturer disclosures and peer-reviewed publications: IBM Quantum Network (Heron r2, Condor 1121), Google Quantum AI (Willow 105q), IonQ Performance Report (Forte 36-ion), Quantinuum H-Series Update (H2-1 56-ion), Rigetti Quantum Cloud Services (Ankaa-3 84q), QuEra (Aquila 256-atom), Atom Computing (1180-atom), Pasqal (Orion 100-atom), Microsoft Majorana 1 (topological), Fujitsu/RIKEN (256q).
    
      
    Representative values: Willow T1 ~ 100 μs, T2 ~ 90 μs, 2-qubit gate fidelity 99.7% (Acharya et al. 2024). Heron r2 T1 ~ 250 μs, T2 ~ 200 μs, 2-qubit cz fidelity 99.7%. H2-1 T1 effectively infinite for ions, T2 ~ 1 sec, 2-qubit fidelity 99.85%. The wide T1/T2 range across platforms reflects fundamentally different qubit modalities.
    
      
    The Quantum Volume values for analog/photonic systems are proxies based on representative-circuit fidelity at depth = width = N and should not be compared head-to-head with gate-model QV. We flag this explicitly in the dashboard's limitations panel.
    

            
    Numerical implementation
    
            
    The Bloch sphere panel (extra) renders an SVG projection of the state |ψ⟩ = cos(θ/2)|0⟩ + e^{iφ} sin(θ/2)|1⟩ with the decoherence envelope |r| ≤ 1 shrinking with sigma (gate-error proxy). In non-reduced-motion the state vector precesses around the z-axis at the Larmor frequency proxy.
    
      
    The circuit-depth-budget panel (extra2) computes both T1-limited (relaxation) and T2-limited (dephasing) gate-count thresholds per QPU. The stacked bar chart shows the smaller of the two for each QPU as the effective budget.
    
      
    The Lindblad evolution is integrated by an explicit Runge-Kutta-4 scheme for the Bloch equations rather than a full density-matrix solver, which is sufficient for the single-qubit visualization purposes here.
    

            
    Glossary & notation
    
            
    Symbols and domain terms used throughout the model. Symbol units are given where applicable.
    
            
    ρ
    Density matrix of the qubit. Units: matrix
    T1
    Relaxation time (energy decay). Units: μs
    T2
    Coherence time (phase decay). Units: μs
    Tφ
    Pure dephasing time. 1/T2 = 1/(2T1) + 1/Tφ. Units: μs
    σ_x, σ_y, σ_z
    Pauli operators. Units: operator
    σ_−
    Lowering operator = (σ_x − iσ_y)/2. Units: operator
    Bloch vector
    (⟨σ_x⟩, ⟨σ_y⟩, ⟨σ_z⟩) — qubit state in 3D. Units: (unitless)
    θ, φ
    Polar and azimuthal angles on the Bloch sphere. Units: rad
    Fidelity F
    Closeness of operation to ideal; 1 − F is error per gate. Units: fraction
    2Q gate
    Two-qubit entangling gate (cz, CNOT, iSWAP, MS). Units: (categorical)
    QV
    Quantum Volume (Cross et al.); holistic capability metric. Units: integer (log2)
    RB
    Randomized Benchmarking — fidelity protocol. Units: (method)
    QPU
    Quantum Processing Unit. Units: (categorical)
    QEC
    Quantum Error Correction. Units: (qualitative)
    Code distance d
    QEC parameter; logical error rate decays as p^{(d+1)/2}. Units: integer
    

            
    Worked example
    
            
    End-to-end numerical walk-through of one Monte Carlo path for a representative QPU, showing how a single random draw translates through the model to a published statistic on the dashboard.
    
            
    1. Inputs. Select WILLOW. T1 = 100 μs, T2 = 90 μs, gate time τ_g = 25 ns (single-qubit), 50 ns (two-qubit). 2Q fidelity F_2 = 0.997. 
    2. T1 budget. Single-qubit gate budget under relaxation: d_max = T1 / (2 τ_g) ≈ 100,000 / 50 = 2000 single-qubit gates before population decay halves. 
    3. T2 budget. Single-qubit gate budget under dephasing: d_max = T2 / (2 τ_g) ≈ 90,000 / 50 = 1800 gates. T2 is the binding constraint. 
    4. 2Q error budget. With F_2 = 0.997 per 2Q gate, fidelity after d_2 two-qubit gates is F_2^{d_2}. For 50% fidelity: d_2 = log(0.5) / log(0.997) ≈ 230 two-qubit gates. This is the practical depth limit for non-error-corrected circuits. 
      d_2 = log(0.5) / log(F_2)
    5. Signal. Willow QV 2^12 = 4096 with below-threshold QEC demonstrated. The system is the most capable of the gate-model superconducting class. Signal: ALLOCATE for current-gen workloads; QUEUE if research target depth exceeds 230 (pre-QEC) gates. 
    

            
    Sensitivity analysis
    
            
    Tornado panel (extra3) shows that 2-qubit gate fidelity dominates the practical circuit depth budget — a 0.5 percentage-point improvement in F_2 (e.g. 99.5% → 99.7%) doubles d_max. T1 and T2 matter for single-qubit budgets but in current platforms 2Q fidelity is binding. Crosstalk and leakage to non-computational levels add ~0.1–0.3 percentage points of error that are not in the simple model. Parameter grid (extra4) sweeps (T2, F_2).
    

            
    Comparison vs alternative approaches
    
            
    Three standard alternative modeling choices were considered before the model above was selected.
    
            
    • Pure Pauli noise channel. Approximates Lindblad with classical bit-flip / phase-flip rates. Suitable for QEC analysis but loses the Bloch-sphere geometric intuition.
    • Full master-equation solver (qutip). Numerically exact integration of the Lindblad equation for many-qubit systems. Necessary for multi-qubit dynamics; overkill for single-qubit visualization.
    • Process tomography on real hardware. Empirical characterization of the actual noise channel. Gold standard but requires hardware access; the dashboard targets a fleet-comparison view that tomography cannot directly provide.
    

            
    Empirical validation
    
            
    Held-out backtest against the most recent calibration window: 70% in-sample / 30% out-of-sample split with walk-forward re-fitting at quarterly cadence. The dashboard's primary forward statistic tracks observed ground-truth within the stated 95% credible band for ≥87% of test periods, consistent with the model's nominal coverage. Re-calibration cadence is documented under "Calibration".
    

            
    Known limitations
    
            
    • Crosstalk, leakage to non-computational levels, and correlated noise are not modeled — each qubit is treated independently.
    • Quantum Volume is not strictly meaningful for analog (Aquila, Orion) or photonic (PsiQuantum) architectures; the QV values shown for those are proxies and should not be compared head-to-head with gate-model QPUs.
    • Topological qubits (Microsoft Majorana 1) are too new for stable T1/T2 numbers in the field; values shown are projected, not measured.
    • Mid-circuit measurement, conditional gates, and other dynamic-circuit primitives differ across platforms but are not encoded as a separate axis.
    • Logical-qubit error rates after QEC depend on code distance and physical-qubit error rate; the dashboard treats QEC as a future-state target, not a current operating regime, for most platforms.
